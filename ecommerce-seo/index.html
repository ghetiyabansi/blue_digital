An Ecommerce SEO Strategy Must Include

1. Keyword research

Have you ever thought about the words your customers might be using to search for products or services like yours? No?! That’s where keyword research comes in.

These words, or keywords, are crucial for your eCommerce business’s success.

Keyword research is an essential step in any successful digital marketing strategy. In order to ensure that your message reaches the right people, it is critical to identify the terms that your target audience is using to search for products and services like yours.

At least 43.0% of all the e-commerce traffic comes from Googles organic traffic.

Conducting in-depth keyword research will help you to identify the most effective words and phrases to use on your website, content, and advertising campaigns.

When conducting keyword research, make sure to target both broad and long-tail keywords.

Broad keywords can be used to capture a wide audience, while long-tail keywords are more specific and typically generate higher-quality leads.

If you own an e-commerce business that sells fresh plants here is what you may do:

Use two broad keywords you may want to include in your SEO plan like “fresh plants online” and “Affordable garden plants” Then, you can branch into long-tail keywords such as Easy to grow plants for sale” or “Affordable Dwarf plants for sale” and “buy best garden plants for beginners.

Each of these long-tail keywords is more specific than the broad ones and is more likely to be used by online shoppers who are looking to invest in an e-commerce service. By targeting these long-tail keywords, you can get noticed by more people who are actually searching for an e-commerce solution and increase your chances of gaining more customers.

There are many content writing services that are available that will help you produce high-quality content for your blogs and get organic traffic.

Here is a detailed video to show you how to do it the right way:

Learn Keyword Research From Experts

By targeting both broad and long-tail keywords, you can maximize your reach and increase conversions.

2. Product optimization

Have you ever landed on a product page that was confusing or difficult to navigate? It’s not a great experience, is it?

As an eCommerce business, it’s crucial that your product pages are optimized to attract and retain customers. This can help you rank higher on SERPs.

Moving up 1 search engine results page (SERP) will boost your click-through rate by an average of 30.8%.

Product optimization is essential for any e-commerce business to ensure that your products are reaching their target audience. It is important to optimize your product pages with relevant keywords, high-quality images, and detailed descriptions in order to maximize their visibility.

Additionally, it is important to make sure your product pages are well-structured and easy to navigate. This helps make sure customers have a positive experience when visiting your site, and are more likely to make a purchase.

Furthermore, it is also important to keep the reader engaged by making the content conversational. By doing this, you can create a more interactive experience for your customers and make them feel like they are part of the conversation.

3. Category optimization

Are your customers having trouble locating the products they want on your eCommerce website due to poorly structured categories?

SEO can take your eCommerce website’s category organization to the next level and boost your search engine visibility at the same time!

Making sure your website’s categories are well-organized and clearly labeled is essential for a successful website. Not only does it make it easier for visitors to find what they’re looking for, but it also helps with SEO.

Source: Ahref

Organizing your website’s categories can be a tedious process, but it is worth the effort in the long run.

  • Start by creating distinct categories that make sense and that are easy to understand.
  • Make sure to label them clearly so visitors can find the content they’re looking for quickly and easily.
  • From there, it’s important to optimize each category page for relevant keywords.
  • Research the keywords that are most likely to be used by potential customers and make sure to include them in your category pages.

Doing this will help your website rank higher in search engine results, making it more visible and increasing the chances of visitors finding and interacting with your content.

4. Technical optimization

Have you ever clicked on a website and had to wait forever for it to load? It’s a frustrating experience, isn’t it?

As an eCommerce business, it’s critical that your website provides a seamless and enjoyable experience for your customers. But if your website is slow to load and difficult to navigate, it can lead to a high bounce rate and lost sales.

Technical optimization is an important part of web design and development that helps to ensure your website is running as efficiently as possible. It can help to guarantee that your website is loading quickly, is mobile-friendly, and has a clear site structure.

Having a website that is optimized for speed and performance is essential in todays digital world. If your website takes too long to load, visitors may become frustrated and leave before they even get to experience your content. By optimizing your websites code and structure, you can greatly improve the speed at which your website loads.

Its also important to make sure your website is optimized for mobile devices. This means creating a design that is responsive to different screen sizes and resolutions. This will ensure that your website looks great and functions properly on any device.

5. Link building

Are you tired of being buried on page 10 of Google’s search results for your eCommerce business?

Low visibility on search engines can make it difficult for your target audience to find your website and ultimately, your products.

This can result in lost sales and missed opportunities for your business.

14.6% of SEO-generated leads are converted into sales.

Link building is an essential part of any successful eCommerce website. Not only does it help your website to become more visible in search engine results, but it also creates a network of other websites that link back to your own, allowing customers to browse your site more easily.

One of the most effective ways to build your link profile is to reach out to other eCommerce websites and ask for backlinks.

Here is a short video for you to learn link building easily:

Learn Link Building With Easy Step-By Step Tutorial

This can be done through email, social media, or even through traditional mail. Make sure that you offer something in return, such as a reciprocal link or a special deal for customers who click through your link.

Another great way to build your backlink profile is to consider guest blogging on relevant sites.

This will not only help to build your backlink profile, but it will also get your brand out to a wider audience. When you write a guest blog post, be sure to include a link back to your own website. This will encourage readers to visit your website and increase your overall traffic. Additionally, you can place links to your website in the author bio section of the blog post.

6. Analytics tracking

Are you tired of making guesses about what marketing tactics will work for your eCommerce business’s online presence?

Do you want to make data-driven decisions for the optimization and success of your eCommerce business?

By using analytics tools, you can gain an understanding of your website’s traffic, rankings, and conversions to help you make informed decisions about your optimization efforts. With analytics tracking enabled, you can track the performance of your website, identify trends and insights, and understand the impact of your SEO efforts.

This information can be used to improve your websites performance and make sure your SEO efforts are delivering results.

Analytics tracking can also provide valuable data on the effectiveness of your SEO efforts. By tracking your websites traffic and rankings, you can determine which keywords and phrases are bringing in the most visitors and helping your website rank higher in search engine results.

This data can then be used to develop more effective SEO strategies. Additionally, analytics tracking can provide insights into which pages on your website are converting visitors and how much revenue each page is generating. This information can help you optimize your website and increase your conversion rates.

By following these specific steps, you can optimize your eCommerce website for search engines and attract more qualified traffic, leading to increased sales and success for your business!

Issue: Site Speed

Solution:One of the few indicators that Google has acknowledged publicly as being used in their algorithm is site speed.

Site performance has a direct influence on your bottom line in addition to being vital for ecommerce SEO. Slow load times have been linked to a 29.8% rise in shopping cart abandonment, according to a study by Radware.

There are several steps you can take to overcome this issue.

  • Change your hosting service provider
  • Compress image files to lessen space consumption
  • Go with a CDN

Issue: Duplicate Content

For ages, duplicate content is one of the most commonly found technical SEO issues with ecommerce sites. There are could be different reasons behind the same.

For example, there might be duplicate URLs on your site which might be having the same content.

Similarly, you may a common content on every page.

Solution:

One thing you can do is to no-index pages that may not bring search engine traffic but cause duplicate content issues.

You can use canonical tags that convey to search engines that specific pages are exact copies or low-level variations of the same page. For search engines, a canonical tag means that the page may not be a unique page.

Moreover, try to write unique and engaging content on each page which is undoubtedly the best way to gain genuine traffic on the search engine results pages.

Issue: Thin Content

Another common technical SEO problem with ecommerce sites is thin content. Therefore, even after you address your issues with duplicate content, you can still have pages with scant material. This is one of the reasons ecommerce websites lose a big chunk of organic traffic.

Solution:

Once you determine the pages with this issue, prepare high-quality, unique content for those pages.

What is Schema for ecommerce and how to implement it?

When you are aiming to enhance your online store’s visibility in search engines, creating a schema plan is essential. Schema is a pathway to provide organized data to search engines like Google, so they have an easier time understanding what type of content is on your website and why it’s there.

Incorporating schema for eCommerce SEO is a great way to increase your website’s visibility and rankings on search engines. Here is a step-by-step technique for getting started:

  1. Start by researching the types of schema markup that pertain to your industry: Schema.org and Google provide organized lists of the various types of schema markup available. If youre selling products, look for schema types such as Product, Offer, and Aggregate Rating.
  2. Create your code for the schema: You can compose it yourself or employ a schema markup generator. No matter which method you choose, make sure your markup is valid and properly formatted for search engine crawlers. Remember that search engines want various sorts of schema markup, so make sure to check what works with each.
  3. Implement your schema markup: Once youve created it, youll need to add it to your website. Depending on your platform, this may vary, but in general, youll want to add the markup to the appropriate template files on your website.
  4. Verify and check your schema markup: After you have implemented it, utilize a testing tool such as the Google Structured Data Testing Tool to make certain that it is running correctly. Additionally, observe your responses as some search engines, like Google, give information on the success of your schema markup.

By adhering to these guidelines and designing a schema marking plan, you can make sure that your website can be accurately identified by search engine robots. This can lead to better search engine positions, greater visibility, and more potential customers. Once you have established the schema marking, ensure that it is constantly updated to achieve the best possible outcomes.
